-Intro to the cube-

The Rubik's Cube, a mesmerizing 3D puzzle that has captivated minds worldwide, was invented by Hungarian architect and professor Ern≈ë Rubik in 1974. Originally conceived as a teaching tool to help students understand three-dimensional geometry, it quickly gained popularity as an addictive and challenging puzzle.

Rubik created the cube by combining his fascination with spatial relationships and geometry. Little did he know that his invention would become one of the most iconic and enduring puzzles of all time. The cube consists of six faces, each made up of nine smaller squares of the same color. The challenge lies in twisting and turning the cube's layers to align the colors on each face.

The Rubik's Cube has evolved from a simple educational tool to a global phenomenon, inspiring competitions, communities, and a vibrant subculture of enthusiasts known as cubers. Its popularity endures as a testament to the timeless appeal of intellectual challenges and the joy of unraveling a seemingly inscrutable puzzle.
